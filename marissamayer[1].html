Marissa Mayer

Mayer was born on May 30, 1975 in Wausau, Wisconsin. She got a bachelors in symbolic systems and a masters in Computer Science from Stanford University. Mayer also researched aritifical intelligence during this time period. She began her career in 1999 as the first female engineer and the 20th employee at Google. She was a key coder in the development of Google Search, Google Maps, Gmail, Google News, Google Earth, Street View and other Google products. She also made a number of inventions relating to computer software such as a program that returned information on articles that related to the search request. She created programs that allowed users to customize their searches and provided them with millions of results within a second. In 2012, Mayer became the CEO of Yahoo, a leading internet service company that had once rivalled Google.
